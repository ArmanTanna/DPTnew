Chamfering all the edges of a selected face of a solid

The Face Chamfer command enables you to chamfer all the edges of a selected face of a solid.

  1. Under Group in the selection list, choose one of the following methods:
    • Distance+45^
    • Distance+Angle
    • Distance+Distance

  2. You are prompted to select a face to chamfer. Click on the face you want to chamfer. To reselect the face, click on Face in the selection list and select the face again.

  3. Specify the chamfer dimensions. The values you must specify vary as follows depending on the method you chose to define the chamfer.
    • If you chose Distance+45^ (to create a 45º chamfer):
      1. In the Distance mini-dialog, specify the distance from the edge.
    • If you chose Distance+Angle:
      1. In the Distance mini-dialog, specify the distance from the edge.
      2. In the Angle mini-dialog, specify the chamfer angle.
    • If you chose Distance+Distance:
      1. In the 1st Distance mini-dialog, specify the first chamfer distance.
      2. In the 2nd Distance mini-dialog, specify the second chamfer distance.

  4. Under More Options select the Keep Edges check box to retain the edges of features intersected by the chamfer



    or clear the check box to extend the chamfer through any intersected edges.



  5. Click or to confirm your selections and chamfer the edges of the face. Click to discard your changes.
