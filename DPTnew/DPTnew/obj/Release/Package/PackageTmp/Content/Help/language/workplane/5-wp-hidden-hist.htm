The Work Plane's hidden history

Even though the Work Plane is not enlisted in the Model Structure, it is part of the history. This article describes how to position the Work Plane in the model and in what way it is linked to objects. This is critical in order to obtain the best results when designing.

Mechanical engineers and all who use the mechanical engineering approach need to be especially aware of how the Work Plane is being positioned. Usually, they need to follow a sort of Work Plane history. Actually, the program manages this automatically, but there are some aspects that you have to be aware of in order to really have your model work in accordance with your design intent. Let's go through an example.

When you start creating a new model, the Work Plane coincides with the Absolute Reference System (World reference system).

  • Sketch a profile and then apply the Linear Solid command to create a solid.



  • Place the Work Plane on the face using the Work Plane on Face or Datum Plane command; notice the "lock" icon. This means that the Work Plane has been linked to the face of the solid.



  • Sketch a profile, and then add a Linear Slot.



  • Move the Work Plane to the center point of the arc. The Work Plane will be linked to that position. Sketch a profile, and then add a Rotational Slot.



  • Place the Work Plane on the corner, and then add a rotation of -45º about the X-axis, using the Select Work Plane command.



  • Sketch a profile, and then add a Linear Slot.



  • Mirror it.


Let's now analyze the history of our Work Plane. We placed the Work Plane on:

  • The World position, and then
  • The 'Face' of the solid (link)
  • The 'Center' of the arc (link)
  • The 'Corner' of the solid (link).
  • Finally, we applied a -45º rotation about the X-axis

What if you did not exactly follow this sequence?
It often happens that you don't exactly follow the intended final sequence of Work Plane placements, because you changed your mind several times (typical design process).

As an example, say you placed the Work Plane on a side face instead of the front face, and then you realized that the right one was the front face, and you eventually placed the Work Plane there. There's an extra step.

No problem. The program does not take into account the unused Work Plane placement events. They will be automatically removed from the hidden history once you Save the file.
