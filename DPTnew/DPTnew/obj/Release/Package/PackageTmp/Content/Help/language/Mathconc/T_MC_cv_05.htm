Circular Helix
  • A circular helix is the curve traced by a point that is rotated about an axis and at the same time translated along the axis.
  • A circular helix may be cylindrical or conical.
  • A turn of a circular helix is the section of the helix between two successive intersections with the same generator of the cylindrical or conical surface on which the helix lies (and of which it is a geodetic).
