Bend Table types and flat length calculation

The think3 application supports seven types of bend tables, corresponding to the different ways of computing the Bend allowance value:

  • K-coeffient (default)
    Bend allowance — and thus development length — is computed based on the K-coefficient values, which are listed in the table.
     
  • K-factor
    Bend allowance — and thus development length — is computed based on the K-factor values, which are listed in the table.
     
  • Bend allowance
    Bend allowance values are listed directly in the bend table. For any angle a, Bend allowance is the product of Bend allowance for 90° bend angle and the a/90 ratio:

    BA = (BA for 90°) . a/90
     
  • Bend deduction
    Bend allowance — and thus development length — is computed based on the Bend deduction values, which are listed in the table.
     
  • Allowance internal
    Bend allowance — and thus development length — is computed based on the internal allowance values, which are listed in the table.
     
  • Allowance external
    Bend allowance — and thus development length — is computed based on the external allowance values, which are listed in the table.
     
  • Internal sharp edge
    Bend allowance — and thus development length — is computed based on the internal sharp edge allowance values, which are listed in the table.
