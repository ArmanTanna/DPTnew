Editing Surfaces

Advanced Surface Continuity Applies positional, tangency or curvature continuity to sets of consecutive surface boundaries and/or curves.
Convert Surface to NURBS Enables you to convert a surface into a NURBS entity.
Draft Surface Applies a draft angle to ruled surfaces.
Invert Surface Modifies a surface by inverting its parameterization.
Normal to Degenerate Surface Enables you to correctly define the normals of degenerate surfaces.
Split Surface Enables you to split a surface along an isoparametric curve.
Surface Continuity Applies positional continuity to two separate surfaces or tangency continuity to two surfaces that have positional continuity at a shared boundary.
Surface Flexibility Enables you to modify the degree and/or continuity of a surface in both parametric directions without changing its shape or position in space.
Surface Shape Constraint Enables you to modify a surface using a handle.
Surface Tension Enables you to apply a controlled deformation to a shape by applying tension to the normal curvature along some controlling curves on the shape.
Surface through Control Points Provides an environment within which you can create and modify curves by governing their control points.
Trim Both Surfaces Trims a sets of surfaces along their intersection with a limit surface.
Trim/Extend Surface Enables you to trim or extend a surface by indicating new parameter ranges or modifying the length along a boundary, while maintaining tangency.
Trim Surface with Limits Trims one or more surfaces of any kind using selected limiting entities.
Untrim Surface Reconstructs the original surface from a surface with parameter limitations.
Split with Silhouette Enables you to split a set of surfaces or solid faces along silhouettes.

Context menu commands

Disable Associativity Enables you to remove associativity from entities that have been promoted to history graph genitors, which is generic root associative curves/surfaces.
Enable Associativity Enables you to apply associativity to non-associative curves and surfaces, thus promoting them to history graph genitors, which is generic root associative curves/surfaces.
